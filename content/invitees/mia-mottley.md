**Your Excellency,**

I extend to you my warmest greetings and highest esteem.

It is with profound respect and great pleasure that I write to invite you personally to be the Guest of Honour and Keynote Speaker at the forthcoming **African Queens and Women Cultural Leaders Summit**, taking place from **14 to 31 August**, a pioneering Pan-African initiative conceived following my installation as a Regnant Queen in 2018.

Your Excellency's statesmanship has transcended the shores of Barbados. Through your fearless advocacy for climate justice, reparatory justice and equitable global development, you have emerged as one of the most influential voices of the Global South and an inspiring champion of people of African descent. Your visionary leadership has restored pride throughout the Caribbean, inspired confidence across Africa and strengthened the enduring bonds between our peoples. **Your steadfast commitment to advancing the cause of reparatory justice and to strengthening the historic bonds between Africa and the Caribbean resonates deeply with the very ethos of this Summit, making Your Excellency's participation both uniquely fitting and profoundly symbolic.** It is precisely because of this remarkable legacy that your presence at this Summit would carry such profound significance.

The Summit is the first initiative of its kind, bringing together African Queens and women cultural leaders to strengthen traditional institutions, build leadership capacity and forge enduring partnerships across Africa and the global African diaspora. Our collective vision is to unite Africa through its traditional leadership while equipping our Queens to receive, guide and reconnect our brothers and sisters in the diaspora as they reclaim their rightful place within the African family.

Confident in the significance and transformative potential of this inaugural Summit, we are already looking beyond it to the international stage. It is my heartfelt aspiration that Barbados, under your distinguished leadership, will become the first nation in the Caribbean to host a future edition of this historic gathering. Given Barbados' unique place in the story of the African diaspora, such an occasion would stand as a powerful symbol of reconnection, healing and shared purpose, creating a legacy that future generations would celebrate with pride.

Your personal attendance at this year's Summit would lend immeasurable distinction to our deliberations. While we would, of course, be honoured to receive any representative you may appoint, I earnestly hope that Your Excellency will consider honouring us with your own presence. Your voice, wisdom and leadership would inspire delegates from across Africa and the diaspora, affirm our shared commitment to Pan-African collaboration and mark a defining moment in strengthening the enduring partnership between Africa and Barbados.

During your visit, it would be my great privilege to welcome you as my personal guest at my British country home in Hawkhill Place, where one of the Summit events will be held. It would provide an opportunity for meaningful dialogue in an atmosphere of friendship, mutual respect and shared purpose, as we explore together new avenues of collaboration between our nations and peoples.

There are occasions that simply deserve representation, and there are occasions that shape history through the presence of visionary leaders. I sincerely believe this is one of the latter. It is my earnest hope that you will accept this invitation and join us in laying another foundation stone in the enduring relationship between Africa and the Caribbean.

I look forward to the singular honour of welcoming Your Excellency and to working together to build a lasting legacy of unity, cultural renaissance and opportunity for the global African family.

Please accept, Your Excellency, the assurances of my highest consideration and profound respect.

**Her Royal Majesty Queen Aruk II**

**Obonganwan Marie Erete**

Founder, African Queens and Women Cultural Leaders Summit
