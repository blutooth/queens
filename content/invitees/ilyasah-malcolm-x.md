**Dear Dr Ilyasah Shabazz,**

I hope this letter finds you well.

It is with great respect and admiration that I write to invite you to serve as a **Keynote Speaker** at the **African Queens and Women Cultural Leaders Summit**, to be held in Oxford and London from **14 to 31 August 2026**, where your keynote address will be delivered at the **University of Oxford**.

In 1964, your father, Malcolm X, delivered his historic address at the Oxford Union, challenging hearts and minds with his unwavering commitment to justice, human dignity and freedom. More than sixty years later, his words continue to inspire generations across the world.

It would be a profound honour to welcome you to Oxford, where your presence would both honour your father's enduring legacy and continue the powerful work you have carried forward through education, dialogue and advocacy.

The Summit brings together African Queens, women cultural leaders, scholars, entrepreneurs and students to celebrate Africa's achievements and to inspire people of African descent worldwide. As a proud woman of African heritage, your voice would resonate deeply with our delegates and reinforce the Summit's vision of unity, leadership and shared progress.

We sincerely hope you will accept this invitation. It would be a privilege to welcome you to Oxford and London, to commemorate your father's legacy while inspiring a new generation of African and global leaders.

During your visit, it would be my personal honour to receive you among the distinguished guests gathered for this historic occasion, in an atmosphere of friendship, dignity and shared purpose.

It would be our privilege to host you, and accommodation will be provided for you at Hawkhill Place, our British country home in Oxford, where several of the Summit engagements will be held.
